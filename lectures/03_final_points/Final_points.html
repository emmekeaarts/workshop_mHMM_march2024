Methodological notes



How much data do we need?

  • Use multivariate data!

  • In case of categorical data, fixed (group-level parameters) only:

  • Low complexity:

    • 400 obs for 2 dep var on 5 subjects
    • 400 obs for 1 dep var in 30 subjects

  • High complexity:

    • 800 obs for 4 dep var on 5 subjects
    • 1600 obs for 1 dep var on 30 subjects

More info: “Go multivariate: Recommendations on Bayesian multilevel HMMs with categorical data” in MBR, https://doi.org/10.1080/00273171/.2023.2205392

Data preprocessing: do sanity checks!

  • Visualize your data
  • EMA / ESM data: check for response shifts, and omit data after shift takes place.
    • Visualizations
    • Objective measures: e.g, very long or short response times and lack of variance in the responses over beeps




Statisticcal notes



Homogeneous paramters over time

  • A common assumption in HMMs is time-homogeneous parameters (also in mHMMbayes).
  • We assume that the process we are studying is generated by the same process over the observation time.
  • Sometimes, obvious we will violate this, for example when starting a study when patients are starting treatment.
  • Solution: include time-varying covaraites. Not implemented in CRAN verson of mHMMbayes, but I do have versions for this.

Starting values

  • Can push the model more in a certain direction compared to weakly informative priors.
  • Reason: inform the first run of the forward-backward algorithm.
  • Use sensible starting values and do convergence checks.
  • When unclear what ‘sensible’ starting values are: obtain paramters when using a frequentist complete pooled approach, for example using the R package depmixS4.
